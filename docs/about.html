How TCU Career Compass Works

This page helps viewers understand what the app is showing, how the datasets are organized, and how to interpret the map, city cards, and quick snapshot insights.

Purpose

TCU Career Compass is a city exploration tool designed to help viewers compare where different career paths may be strongest after graduation. Instead of showing one generic job market, the app organizes city data by major and then by career path so users can explore options that align with their field of study.

How To Use It

1. Choose A Major

Start with the major dropdown. Once a major is selected, the map limits itself to locations connected to that major’s career paths.

2. Choose A Career Path

Career path buttons appear based on the selected major. Clicking one loads that specific dataset and updates the city list, insights, and map to match it.

3. Explore The Results

Users can compare top cities, read quick snapshot summaries, and click map markers to open location popups with occupation and wage details.

Reading the Map

How Cities Are Ranked: Cities are ranked by adjusted annual mean wage (cost-of-living adjusted). This metric identifies the strongest financial opportunities because it reflects real earning power across different regional markets.

What You See on the Map: Each city is marked with either a star or circle. Stars identify the best-paying city for each occupation. Circles represent all other cities, colored by their wage percentile rank:

Interacting with Markers: Click any marker to see detailed information: metro name, occupation, wages, employment, location quotient, and RPP.

Why Stars vs. Circles: Stars use bright purple (#7c3aed) to highlight the highest-paying city for each specific occupation. This makes the best opportunity stand out visually. Circles show all other cities, ranked by wage percentile.

What The Data Means

Metro

The metro field is the full metropolitan statistical area used as the location label throughout the app.

Annual Mean Wage

This is the average yearly wage for the related occupation in that metro area.

Employment

Employment shows how many people work in that occupation within the metro area.

Location Quotient

Location quotient compares how concentrated an occupation is in a metro relative to the national average. Higher values suggest stronger specialization.

Regional Price Parity (RPP)

RPP is a 2024 BEA cost-of-living index (100 = national average). States with RPP > 100 are more expensive; states with RPP < 100 are less expensive. Used to adjust wages for fair comparison across regions.

Adjusted Annual Mean Wage

This is the annual mean wage adjusted for regional cost-of-living differences using RPP. It shows what the salary is worth in purchasing power relative to the national average, enabling fair salary comparisons across different cost-of-living areas.

Occupation

Each career path can be connected to a BLS-style occupation label, such as Management Analysts or Data Scientists.

How The Quick Snapshot Works

Highest Salary

The city with the largest raw annual wage for your career path. This shows the nominal salary before adjusting for regional cost-of-living differences.

Best Adjusted Wage

The city with the largest adjusted wage after accounting for regional cost-of-living (RPP). This represents the strongest financial opportunity in terms of actual purchasing power and is used to rank the city list and highlight top locations on the map.

Highest Location Quotient

The city with the largest location_quotient, meaning the strongest occupation concentration in that field.

How The Files Are Organized

The datasets are grouped by major inside the data folder. Each major has its own folder, and each career path points to one JSON file. This keeps the project scalable and makes it easier to swap in updated datasets later.
